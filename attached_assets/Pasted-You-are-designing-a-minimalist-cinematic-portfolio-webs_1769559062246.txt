You are designing a minimalist cinematic portfolio website 
for an independent creative IP studio.

This website is NOT a corporate homepage.
It is an atmospheric showcase of a world.

Core references:
- A24 official website (mood-first, minimal explanation)
- arthouse cinema aesthetics
- slow pacing, silence, restraint

────────────────────────
STRUCTURE
────────────────────────

Global Navigation:
- Film
- Fiction
(no other main menus)

No “About Us”, no mission statements, no marketing copy.

────────────────────────
VISUAL & UX RULES
────────────────────────

- Dark or desaturated background (off-black, deep blue, muted gray)
- Generous negative space
- Typography: elegant serif or restrained sans-serif
- No bright colors
- No rounded “startup” UI
- No excessive animation

Scroll behavior:
- Slow, deliberate
- Sections feel like scenes, not pages
- Transitions should feel cinematic, not playful

Images and videos take priority over text.

────────────────────────
HOMEPAGE
────────────────────────

Opening screen:
- Fullscreen image or silent looping video
- One short sentence only (maximum 12 words)
- Sentence must feel like a philosophical statement, not a slogan

Examples (do not reuse literally):
“This world does not explain itself.”
“Something ordinary is quietly wrong.”

No buttons on first screen.
Scroll to proceed.

────────────────────────
FILM SECTION
────────────────────────

Layout:
- Grid or vertical list of film works
- Each entry shows:
  - Title
  - Year
  - One still image or short loop

No plot summaries.
No cast lists.
No genre tags.

Optional interaction:
- On hover or click, reveal ONLY:
  - One sentence (abstract, atmospheric)
  - Duration

Films should feel connected by tone, even if stories differ.

────────────────────────
FICTION SECTION
────────────────────────

Purpose:
- Showcase written worlds as intellectual IP, not blog posts.

Layout options:
- Titles floating in space
- Or vertical column with heavy spacing

Each fiction entry includes:
- Title
- Year
- One fragment of text (1–2 sentences maximum)

Rules:
- Do NOT show full texts.
- Do NOT use synopses.
- Text must feel incomplete, like a fragment or observation.

────────────────────────
CONTACT
────────────────────────

Contact must be subtle and optional.

- No contact form.
- Email address or minimal link only.
- Example placement: footer, low-contrast text.

Wording example tone (do not copy literally):
“For those who wish to enter.”

────────────────────────
IMPORTANT CONSTRAINTS
────────────────────────

- Do NOT explain the world.
- Do NOT optimize for SEO.
- Do NOT include social media icons.
- Do NOT include calls to action like “Submit”, “Buy”, “Subscribe”.

The site should feel like:
- an archive
- a signal
- an invitation without persuasion

Audience:
- curators
- producers
- collaborators
- institutions

The goal is not clarity.
The goal is trust through consistency of tone.
